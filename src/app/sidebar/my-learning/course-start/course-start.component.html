This document provides a foundational overview of performance fees in fund accounting, focusing on how and when they are applied in investment funds. It covers key concepts such as Gross Asset Value (GAV), Net Asset Value (NAV), and the importance of calculating performance fees based on accurate, fee adjusted values.
Core components like the High Water Mark (HWM) are explained ensuring that fund managers only earn fees when surpassing previous value peaks and the Hurdle Rate, which sets a minimum required return before fees apply. Through examples and a knowledge check, the material illustrates how performance fees are accrued only when a fund is “in performance.” The document emphasizes the need to understand fund specific terms and encourages diligence in reading fund documentation, particularly when hurdle rates or benchmarks like LIBOR or S&P 500 are involved.
