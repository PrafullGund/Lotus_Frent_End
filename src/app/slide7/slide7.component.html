Performance Fees Basic Concept – High Water Mark

What Is a High-Water Mark?

A high-water mark “HWM” is the highest peak in value that an investment fund or class has reached.

Typically it will be higher of

A. the high point reach at the end of a performance period

Or

A. The initial subscription amount

This term is often used in the context of fund manager compensation, which is performance-based. The high-water mark ensures the manager does not get paid if the fund/class doesn’t get above that level.

If the manager loses money over a period, he must get the fund above the high-water mark before receiving a performance fee from the assets under management (AUM).
